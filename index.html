Scrabble is a word game for two to four players in which points are scored by placing tiles with single letters onto a playing board divided into 1515 squares. The tiles must create words that are contained in a standard dictionary or lexicon and read left to right in rows or downward in columns in a crossword format.

Alfred Mosher Butts, an American architect, devised the game in 1938. Hasbro manufactures Scrabble in the United States and Canada under the names of both of its companies, Milton Bradley and Parker Brothers. Outside of the United States and Canada, Mattel holds the rights to produce Scrabble. The game is offered in 121 countries and is available in over 30 languages; around 150 million sets have been sold globally, and approximately one-third of American and half of British households own a Scrabble set.There are over 4,000 Scrabble clubs worldwide.
